Second Harvest Heartland Design #2

As Design Team Lead and contributor for a volunteer recruitment campaign with Second Harvest Heartland, I created 5 reusable promotional graphics to help food banks across Minnesota attract more volunteers, specifically bilingual individuals. This design was developed as a reusable social media asset designed to foster volunteer appreciation and community engagement. This post highlights individual contributions in a warm, playful style that reflects the food bank’s welcoming environment, supporting volunteer retention through recognition. I determined our team should utilize Canva for its accessibility and low-resource adaptability for the clients. I applied industry-standard practices such as visual hierarchy, strategic use of white space, and a welcoming brand kit which included typography, and a unified color scheme which would serve to unify the team's designs. The design features a market-stand-inspired header and a quote bubble, balancing graphic charm with professionalism. Shadow effects and color contrast were used to emphasize key elements like the volunteer’s name and quote, to catch eyes attention and readability. Although not a direct call to action, the piece is strategically tailored to affirm volunteers and promote social media engagement, cultivating a sense of belonging while passively encouraging others to join. This approach aligned with our research for best practices in nonprofit marketing.
